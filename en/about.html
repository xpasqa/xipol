About Political Science

Faculty of Social and Political Sciences UIN Bandung

The Political Science Program under the Faculty of Social and Political Sciences (FISIP) UIN Sunan Gunung Djati Bandung was officially established in 2018 based on the Decree of the Director General of Islamic Education No. 283/KPT/I/2018. Since its inception, this academic program has emerged as part of the wider mandate scheme of UIN Sunan Gunung Djati Bandung, marking the expansion of scholarly domains beyond traditional Islamic studies.

From its inception, the Political Science Program has demonstrated unwavering commitment to academic excellence. This commitment was validated in 2021 when the program achieved "Good" accreditation from the National Accreditation Board for Higher Education (BAN-PT) through Decree No. 13549/SK/BAN-PT/Akred/S/XII/2021. In 2022, a comprehensive curriculum revision was implemented to align with contemporary political science developments and FISIP's academic framework.

The program has been led by two Department Heads:
• Dr. H. Asep A. Sahid, M.Si. (2018–2023)
• Dr. Hasan Mustapa, S.Fil.I., M.Si. (2023–present)

To ensure sustainability and enhance competitiveness, the Political Science Program actively promotes faculty and staff development through curriculum enhancement, pedagogical innovation, and intensified research and community engagement activities. The program also facilitates faculty participation in local, national, and Southeast Asian academic forums, while establishing training and empowerment centers to strengthen students' soft skills.

Vision & Mission

Vision

To become an excellent and competitive Political Science Program in Southeast Asia based on Rahmatan lil 'Alamin by 2029.

Mission

  1. To conduct education, research, and community service relevant to social dynamics at local, national, and Southeast Asian levels.
  2. To prepare graduates with mental, spiritual, and intellectual resilience in political science.
  3. To develop innovation and adaptation to advances in science and technology to enhance graduate independence.

Objectives

  1. To implement educational, research, and community service processes that address socio-political challenges at various levels.
  2. To develop political science graduates who are academically, spiritually, and morally resilient.
  3. To produce graduates capable of empowering society toward democratic and just governance.

Graduate Profiles

Graduates of the Political Science Program at UIN Sunan Gunung Djati Bandung are designed to meet the needs of the professional world and society through four main profiles:

Political Researcher

Conducting theory-based and political ethics studies and publishing them in academic forums.

Public Policy Analyst

Analyzing and formulating public policies based on data and governance principles.

Political Consultant

Designing campaign strategies and providing political insights based on research and social dynamics.

Political Communicator

Building political communication through content, media, and public advocacy in an ethical manner.

Alumni Achievements

Graduates of the Political Science Program at UIN Bandung have played strategic roles across various important sectors in national development. They actively serve as election organizers at the General Election Commission (KPU), Election Supervisory Agency (Bawaslu), and polling station levels, as well as legislative expert staff, civil servants in government institutions, and civil society activists in various NGOs. Their sharp analytical capabilities and contextual understanding of politics have earned graduates trust in public policy formulation and implementation.

Many alumni have pursued master's degree education domestically and internationally through LPDP, Ministry of Religious Affairs, and other institutional scholarships. They contribute as academics, researchers, and political consultants who provide intellectual contributions on issues of democracy, governance, and human rights. This trajectory demonstrates that Political Science graduates are not only intellectually adaptive but also actively engaged in scientific and policy development schemes.

Additionally, several alumni have emerged as political communicators and social influencers who shape public opinion through media, digital campaigns, and social movements. They represent a new generation of critical, communicative, and integrity-driven young Muslim intellectuals who champion values of justice, diversity, and participation. Alumni achievements reflect the synergy between academic competence, spirituality, and strong social commitment.

Tridharma and Higher Education

Education serves as the primary pillar implemented through lectures, academic discussions, and project-based interactive learning. The Political Science Program strives to develop students who not only understand theory but are also capable of applying it in real socio-political contexts. The curriculum is designed to meet contemporary needs and remain relevant to local challenges and Southeast Asian regional issues.

Research is developed as a means of strengthening the scientific capacity of students and faculty. The program actively promotes research collaboration between faculty and students through internal research schemes, external partnerships, and participation in national and international academic forums. This enables students to be directly involved in data-based academic processes and critical analysis.

Community service is implemented through community-based social programs, training, policy advocacy, and political literacy activities. Tridharma is not merely a formal obligation but serves as the foundation for character development and the Political Science Program's tangible contribution to building a participatory and democratic society.

Facilities and Infrastructure

Political Science students at UIN Bandung receive comprehensive facility support that maximally enhances the teaching and learning process. These include the Language Laboratory and Media Laboratory, which serve as essential facilities for mastering communication and political media analysis.

Beyond academic facilities, student organizations and associations are available that actively promote the development of soft skills, leadership, and social networks. These organizational activities are integrated with character development and student professionalism in the real world.

Generally, the UIN Bandung campus is also equipped with various modern facilities such as a central library, campus mosque, internet and Wi-Fi services throughout the area, career service center, sports center, as well as access to health and psychological services for students.
